3 Sites for accessing publications and digital editions

Also includes things like catalogues and research databases to prevent header clutter. I did consider sorting these, or having a separate heading for general vs. subfield specific sites, but, I fear this will make the page harder to navigate.

  • arhive.org
  • baekur.is. A catalogue with online editions of old Icelandic books.
  • Das Münchener Digitalisierungszentrum.
  • handrit.is. A catalogue of Icelandic and Norse manuscripts.
  • Institut for Nordiske Studier og Sprogvidenskabs digitale samlinger. See these pages for the Arnamagnæan collection, dialect research, and name research. Related links:
    • Danske dialekter, sprog og sproglig forskellighed.
    • Håndskrifter. A page on the Scandinavian manuscripts and manuscript culture of the Middle Ages. Of prime interest are the timeline and the browsable manuscripts, but the other links accessible from the main page are also useful.
    • A page on names.
    • A page on place names.
    • The Institute for Nordic Studies’ homepage (in Danish), linked above also. Contains many of the same links, and some other ones. The site isn’t very intuitive for me to navigate - the digital collections page seems to duplicate some of the content elsewhere; I’d recommend doing systematic searches of the pages relevant to you.
  • Nasjonalbiblioteket.
  • The Royal Danish Library’s e-manuscripts.
  • Stories for all time: The Icelandic fornaldarsögur. A research project based at The Arnamagnæan Institute surverying the entire transmission history of the fornaldarsögur norðurlanda. Includes a catalogue of all the manuscripts with information about them, as well as a bibliography of secondary sources, translations, and editions.

5.1.1 Welsh

  • Geiriadur Prifysgol Cymru. The Welsh answer to the OED - and hence the principle historical and etymological dictionary of Welsh. 6.1.3 Reference grammars and tools

6.1.3.1 Icelandic

  • Árnarson, Kristján, Kvaran, Guðrún, and Þráinsson, Höskuldur (eds.; 2013). Íslensk tunga. Reykjavík: Almenna Bókafélagið. The most extensive grammar of the modern language.
  • Beygingarlýsing íslensks nútímamáls (BÍN). Stofnun Árna Magnússonar í íslenskum fræðum. Available at: https://bin.arnastofnun.is/.

6.1.4 Dictionaries

While it is not a traditional dictionary, the site Glosbe can be very useful - it effectively collates translated subtitles.

6.1.4.1 Danish

  • Den Danske Ordbog: Moderne danske sprog. The cut-off point for ‘modern’ is ca. 1950. Available at: https://ordnet.dk/ddo.
  • Ordbog over det danske Sprog. Covers the period 1700-1950. Available at https://ordnet.dk/ods.

6.1.4.2 Faroese

  • sprotin.fo. Hosts a monolingual and many bilingual Faroese dictionaries, includes inflectional/declensional information (not a given - usually in dictionaries are given, say, nom.sg., gen.sg., and nom.pl. or whatever else is needed to figure out the full declension yourself - which is fine, but perhaps intimidating to a casual browser.)

6.1.4.3 Icelandic

Many of these are available on snara.is for a subscription fee; it also collates some freely available elsewhere.

  • Böðvarsson, Árni, Árnarson, Mörður. Íslensk Orðabók. Reykjavík: Forlagið. This and the next one are the two most exhaustive, I believe.
  • Jónsdottir, Halldóra, Úlfarsdóttir, Þórdis et al. Íslensk nútímamálsorðabók. Reykjavík: Stofnun Árna Magnússonar. Available at https://islenskordabok.arnastofnun.is/.
  • Hólmarsson, Sverrir, Sanders, Christopher, Tucker, John (2009). Íslensk-ensk orðabók. Iðunn. Available at http://digicoll.library.wisc.edu/IcelOnline/Search.TEId.html.
  • Úlfarsdóttir, Þórdís (aðalritstj.). ISLEX. Reykjavík: Stofnun Árna Magnússonar. A multilingual dictionary by the Stofnun Árna Magnússonar, the Dansk Sprog- og Litteraturselskab, the Institutt for lingvistiske, litterære og estiske studier, the Institution för svenska språket, the Fróðskaparsetur Føroya, and the Helsingin yliopisto. Available at: https://islex.arnastofnun.is/is/.
  • Magnússon, Ásgeir Blöndal. Íslensk orðsifjabók. Available at https://malid.is/.
  • Jónsson, Jón Hilmar (2005). Stóra orðabókin um íslenska málnotkun. Reykjavík: JPV Útgáfa. Useful as a learner for checking one’s grammar.
  • Friðjónsson, Jón (2012). Mergur málsins: Íslensk orðatiltæki, uppruni, saga og notkun. Reykjavík: Örn og örlygur. If this one is of interest/use to you, see Orð að sönnu. The pulisher may be wrong, as it is now published online and snara.is doesn’t list the current publisher.
  • Friðjónsson, Jón. (2019). Orð að sönnu: Íslenskir málshættir og orðskviðir. Forlagið/Snara.

6.1.4.4 Norse

  • Cleasby-Vigfússon. An Icelandic-English Dictionary. Deals primarily with prose vocabulary. Don’t let the title fool you. Text here: http://www.germanic-lexicon-project.org/texts/oi_cleasbyvigfusson_about.html
  • Fritzner, J. Ordbog over det gamle norske sprog. Deals primarily with prose vocabulary. Available to search here: https://www.edd.uio.no/perl/search/search.cgi?appid=86&tabid=1275 or to browse/download here: https://archive.org/details/ordbogoverdetga05fritgoog. You can get it in print still, as well, I believe it usually comes in two volumes in that case.
  • Ordbog over det norrøne prosasprog. Aims to be an exhaustive dictionary of Norse prose. Incomplete.
  • Sveinbjörn, E. and Jónsson, F. Lexicon poeticum antiquæ linguæ septentrionalis. Available here: http://www.septentrionalia.net/lex/ordbog2.pdf. Sometimes this link is down; you should be able to find the same page on archive.org if that is the case.
  • The new Lexicum Poeticum. Very much a work in progress but extremely useful. https://lexiconpoeticum.org/m.php?p=lp
  • Baetke, Walter. Wörterbuch zur altnordischen Prosaliteratur. (Warning: PDF link.)
  • Gammeldansk Ordbog. Det Danske Sprog- og Litteraturselskab.
  • Kalkar, Otto. Ordbog til det ældre danske sprog.
  • Fornsvensk lexikalisk databas.

6.1.4.5 Norwegian

  • Bokmålsordboka og Nynorskordboka. Available at: https://ordbok.uib.no/.
  • Norsk Ordbok: Ordbok over det norske folkemålet og det nynorske skriftmålet. Available at: http://no2014.uib.no/perl/ordbok/no2014.cgi.
  • Det Norske Akademis ordbok. Available at: https://naob.no/. Some of the word usage is nearer Riksmål than Bokmål.
  • Haugen, Einar (1974). Norsk-engelsk ordbok. Uni. of Wisconsin Press. Has Bokmål, Nynorsk, and dialect; some of it is quite dated, but it is useful in that it can have words hard to find elsewhere.
  • The dictionaries hosted at https://ordnett.no/. Particularly useful are the Stor engelsk ordbok, the Teknisk engelsk ordbok, the Bokmål rettskrivningsordbok, the Nynorsk rettskrivingsordbok, and the Stor norsk ordbok. Maybe the Norsk Riksmålsordbok as well, but I do not have access to this one.
  • Store Norske Leksikon. Available at: https://snl.no/. Technically an encyclopedia, but still useful to consult.

6.1.4.6 Swedish

  • Svenska Akadamiens Ordböcker. Available at: https://svenska.se/. Includes the Svensk Akademis ordlista, which acts as an inofficial norm for the spelling and inflection of modern Swedish (while also containing some information about word meanings), Svensk ordbok, a dictionary of modern Swedish (with information on word history), and Ordbok, a historical dictionary of the written language from 1521 to the present.

6.1.5.1 Danish

8 Indo-Aryan

  • Reinöhl, Uta. (2016). Grammaticalization and the Rise of Configurationality in Indo-Aryan. OUP. ‘The overarching hypothesis of this study, that the grammaticalization of postpositions entails the emergence of phrase structure, can be elaborated into the following three principle claims:
    1. The rise of the modern postpositional phrases hinges on the grammaticalization of the individual postpositions. […].
    2. Postpositional phrases in Indo-Aryan develop from asymmetrical constructions. […]
    3. The formation of syntactic phrases is triggered by semantic changes involving metaphorical as well as metonymic transfers from the literal meaning of the constructions in question.’ (p. 50)
